My identity & values

Values verse beliefs?

Values are an interesting subject, they are or can be subjective, what seperates them from belief or beliefs, how can you accurately gauge these things. Can you truly self reflect or analyse, to what level? Can you truly identify your values or are your values more like an opinion. How you see your self, how you would like to see your self, how you truly are, how other people see you, or maybe how you would like to be seen? Your actions, your reactions, your experience / experiences what you've chosen / what you've had to or just have experienced, and so on.

Things I'd like to believe I value are - honestly, competency, curiosity, respect, love, integriy, commitment / heart, responsibility, trust, creativity and truth. This is just scratching the surface, if I look at a list of values I indentify with a lot of them. Also not having a set limit somewhat makes this even more difficult, although I guess narrowing it down is also similar, and it's been hinted to provide evidence for these values. So upon reading this, this exercise started to make a little more sense to me, though I don't think I'll truly get to the bottom of this without far more time spent.

A story from highschool that relates, or related to my integrity and honesty. I was 18 at Welington High School when I found a $20 note at lunch time, I wondered what to do, but I ended up handing it in to the office. I can tell you most of my friends would not have done the same. Hillariously it actually got back to the rightful owner and I met them at a party years later, they knew my name as well and I'd never met them, small Wellington as they say.

If I were to think about an achievement I'm proud of, it would probably be my degree / bachelors in jazz performance. At the same time, I also didn't really care about the piece of paper, but have found it hasn't exactly hindered me, especially when it came to teaching.

A quote that I like to live by is "You miss 100% of the shots you don't take." Also "Expect everything and anything is nothing, expect nothing and anything is everything." There are lots of great quotes and many I relate to but these come to me straight off the top of my head, without needing to find them.

My Strengths

Well I have taken the big 5. this result seems to somewhat reflect how I see my self, whether accurate or not is obviously another question entirely. Althought this test has a lot of what I would personally consider, negative connotations, that do not necessarily reflect reality.

Through my own personal development I can tell you that my strengths are changing lenses, being persistent and trying to get the most out of what I'm doing.

My weaknesses

My Weaknesses are viewing the whole thing or big picture. Say when I was at university I would think about writing the entire essay and all that that entailed even though logically I knew, just write a paragraph a day, just get something started etc. My default was to view the whole task then be overwhelmed. I obviously was able to overcome this, and would just write anything, then eventually got a frame work / system, start writing a draft remind my self it doesn't matter how bad it is I can always make it better etc.

I can tend to be harsh with my words at times, not is a nasty way but in a cut through to the core issue or point. Over the years I have learnt more EQ and how to communicate feelings and in more suitable tones when necessary.

Ethical decision

There is the money story above. I didn't weigh up the values I just did what I believed was right. I reflect positively on it, and would like to believe I'd make the same or similar decision again, though times have changed and unfortunately, money is far more necessary. I would like to think I'd still make the same decision.

Another situation that probably works better for this prompt, though I feel massive cringe telling it ( and haven't thought of a better one yet!!), would be the decision to end a relationship.
I had realised we didn't make each other happy, and I think we were both together because neither of us wanted to be alone, we were cruising on the attraction to each other. I think part of the problem was on paper we were a successful realtionship, or at least we thought so.

I weighed up many angles, beliefs & values from commitment, to truth, and what I think we both held onto the most, love.

The pros were definitely in favour in the long run, but in the short run it seemd like a loss, and well, it was. I decidied to start talking about things we hadn't really discussed - kids, where we would end up living, what I'd need to earn etc. I also brought up stuff we had been mulling over, I knew these talks would most likely kill the relationship, and that was part of the problem. The fact that these important talks had never really occured, and even worse that they could destroy the relationship. There was no real foundation. Looking back it's so obvious, but at the time, inside it, it felt real, important, strong.
Ultimately this discussion did lead to the end of the realtionship, though I had hoped it would have ended on better terms than it did at the time.

I reflect positively on the decision now, we didn't end on the best note then but we are are on better terms now, but that took years. As it quite often does.

I honestly think we should have broken up earlier, but everything is always easier in hindsight, and you don't know what you don't know. Let alone the main point that neither of us really wanted to face reality, and that be can easier to avoid then one might think ( at least in my experience ).

My culture, and its influence

I suppose a cultural aspect that has influenced me was that from age like 9 or something, I was allowed a little glass of wine with dinner. This led me to never feeling like I wasn't aloud to drink, and I barely went through the crazy drinking stages most of my friends did. I say barely because I'd be lieing if I said I didn't have the odd party night when I drunk far more than I should have.

The strengths to guide my journey

My main strengths are drawn from studying music, I have a true understanding of how I'll never know everything and I can always learn more. This will help me stay focussed and never get an ego.

Another strength will be looking at problems from multiple angles, and the humility to realise I might have in fact overlooked something increadibly basic. This will help me solve and trouble shoot problems and interactions.

My limitations

I will need to be more consistent in terms of even circadian rhythm. I'm already working on this, but this will help me in many ways.

Though aware of it, I will need to make sure I tackle problems with the right framework, and avoid trying to look at the whole problem if it is unrealistic.

I will need to take breaks, and make sure I actually do, as I can get fixated and focus too much on something, this can also make me irritable if I'm itterupted, which can be added to if I haven't eaten properly.

Conflict resolution

I've had numerous experiences like this, some even in drumline. One time there was a lot of resistance around doing something a particular way. I outlined some of the reasons why I thought we should do it this way, and even entertained a vote. Another time I had to ask for clarification from, I guess you'd call them management. I think the management stragey should be a last resort most of the time. I found explaining why to most people helps, and also where possible letting people have an input like a vote or feed back / a chance to voice their concerns, these were the most effective strategies in my opinion.

Final reflection

It is interesting to look at this from an outside perspective, I've really looked deeply into my strengths and weaknesses, but I've never really delved into values much. I have obviously looked into beliefs, outlooks, lense's etc and well you could argue values sit in that.

There's always more to learn as they say.

EQ and IQ

What is Emotional intelligence?

From the oxford dictionary: "the capacity to be aware of, control, and express one's emotions, and to handle interpersonal relationships judiciously and empathetically. "emotional intelligence is the key to both personal and professional success""

A lot of definitions are very similar to this: "Emotional Intelligence (EI) is the ability to manage both your own emotions and understand the emotions of people around you. There are five key elements to EI: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills." From Mental Health America.

The main themes are:

  1. Self-awareness
  2. self-management or self-regulation
  3. Motivation ( though some definitions leave this out..)
  4. Empathy ( some also miss this out and or phrase it differently )
  5. social skills or social awareness, and or relationship management.

A lot of the benefits are said to be around having lower levels or stress, being less emotionally reactive and being more aware of the people around you and your impact on each other.

The way I personally see emotional inteligence or EQ is like a person who aims to be stoic ( stoicism ), whilst also being able to 'read' body language. Emotional inteligence is thought to be a learned skill. Though some people will start at different levels, especially with aspects of reading peoples moods feelings etc.

My weaknesses

My Weaknesses are viewing the whole thing or big picture. Say when I was at university I would think about writing the entire essay and all that that entailed even though logically I knew, just write a paragraph a day, just get something started etc. My default was to view the whole task then be overwhelmed. I obviously was able to overcome this, and would just write anything, then eventually got a frame work / system, start writing a draft remind my self it doesn't matter how bad it is I can always make it better etc.

I can tend to be harsh with my words at times, not is a nasty way but in a cut through to the core issue or point. Over the years I have learnt more EQ and how to communicate feelings and in more suitable tones when necessary.

My culture, and its influence

I suppose a cultural aspect that has influenced me was that from age like 9 or something, I was allowed a little glass of wine with dinner. This led me to never feeling like I wasn't aloud to drink, and I barely went through the crazy drinking stages most of my friends did. I say barely because I'd be lieing if I said I didn't have the odd party night when I drunk far more than I should have.

The strengths to guide my journey

My main strengths are drawn from studying music, I have a true understanding of how I'll never know everything and I can always learn more. This will help me stay focussed and never get an ego.

Another strength will be looking at problems from multiple angles, and the humility to realise I might have in fact overlooked something increadibly basic. This will help me solve and trouble shoot problems and interactions.

My limitations

Conflict resolution

I've had numerous experiences like this, some even in drumline. One time there was a lot of resistance around doing something a particular way. I outlined some of the reasons why I thought we should do it this way, and even entertained a vote. Another time I had to ask for clarification from, I guess you'd call them management. I think the management stragey should be a last resort most of the time. I found explaining why to most people helps, and also where possible letting people have an input like a vote or feed back / a chance to voice their concerns, these were the most effective strategies in my opinion.

Final reflection

It is interesting to look at this from an outside perspective, I've really looked deeply into my strengths and weaknesses, but I've never really delved into values much. I have obviously looked into beliefs, outlooks, lense's etc and well you could argue values sit in that.

There's always more to learn as they say.

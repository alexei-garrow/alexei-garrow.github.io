My learning plan

My Goal

My long term goal is to get to the point of being able to contract on my terms. I see the career pathway as learning through this course, then starting out as a an intern / junior developer, learning the ropes and slowly climbing the ladder. My main goal is to be good enough to contract within 5 years after this course.

My strengths and limitations

My strengths when it comes to learning, are mainly around the goals and motivation of watning to learn this stuff, bringing a dedication and commitment. I can analyse things to a certain extent, I'm hoping this will help me learn things faster and on a deeper level.

My limitations when it comes to learning are: Primarily about time management, self discipline, and routine. I have lot's of room to grown with time management / scheduling etc. I have strong self discipline, but when it's related to routine or keeping one I am more lax than I should be.

The main thing is routine, this is my biggest hurdle, creating new habits and keeping them. Life for me has never had a truly set routine, it's always changed depending on what I was doing. There is always the posibility of a last minute call to a gig with a late start / end time. That won't change as much, but the main factor is developing a consistent circadian rhythm. A true baseline to function from.

I can go through periods of months with a set routine, then roll my sleep pattern. That can sometimes take a few days to flip around agin. Normally with teaching when this happened it would be during the school holidays, when I didn't have commitments. It didn't always happen, but sometimes the results were far from ideal. I have learnt a lot about myself, and basic psychology as I delved deeper into this stuff.

The 2 main things to prevent this are a set end time for work, that allows some time to relax or chill out, and a discipline about a max bed time, with a proper wind down ( ie no phone or sleep hygiene as they call it ). The other less obvious factor I have found is reinging in my FOMO ( fear of missing out ), especially with friends who's only hobbys are video games etc, I can't practically keep up with these things ( I have a competitive nature around these things, that doesn't spill over into real life anywhere to this level.).

Core growth

I have done a lot of work on myself over the years, and much of it was in this area. That said there's always room to improve. I'd like to keep a stoic head whilst being an empathetic person. I'll need to develop my professional communication and skills around these areas, as it's been a while since I've had an 'office job'.

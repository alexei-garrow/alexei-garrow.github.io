My learning plan

My Goal

My long term goal is to get to the point of being able to contract on my terms. I see the career pathway as learning through this course, then starting out as a an intern / junior developer, learning the ropes and slowly climbing the ladder. My main goal is to be good enough to contract within 5 years after this course.

My strengths and limitations

My strengths when it comes to learning, are mainly around the goals and motivation of wanting to learn this stuff, bringing a dedication and commitment. I can analyse things to a certain extent, I'm hoping this will help me learn things faster, and on a deeper level.

My limitations when it comes to learning are primarily about time management, self discipline, and routine. I have lot's of room to grown with time management / scheduling etc. I have strong self discipline, but when it's related to routine or keeping one, I am more lax than I should be.

The main thing is routine, this is my biggest hurdle, creating new habits and keeping them. Life for me has never had a truly set routine, it's always changed depending on what I was doing. There is always the posibility of a last minute call, a gig with a late start / end time. That won't change as much, but the main factor is developing a consistent circadian rhythm. A true baseline to function from.

I can go through periods of months with a set routine, then roll my sleep pattern. That can sometimes take a few days to flip around again. Normally with teaching when this happened it would be during the school holidays, when I didn't have commitments. It didn't always happen, but sometimes the results were far from ideal. I have learnt a lot about myself, and basic psychology as I delved deeper into this stuff.

The two main things to prevent this are: A set end time for work, that allows some time to relax or chill out, and a discipline about a max bed time, with a proper wind down ( ie no phone or sleep hygiene as they call it ). The other less obvious factor I have found is reigning in my FOMO ( fear of missing out ), especially with friends who's only hobbys are video games etc. I can't practically keep up with these things ( I have a competitive nature around these things, that doesn't spill over into real life anywhere near this videogame level.).

Core growth

I have done a lot of work on myself over the years, and much of it was in this area. That said there's always room to improve. I'd like to keep a stoic head whilst being an empathetic person. I'm always looking to build upon my communication skills. I can sometimes do with reading the room a little better. I'll need to develop my professional communication and skills around these areas, as it's been a while since I've had an 'office job'.

My commitment to Dev Academy

My main commitment to this programme in general is to give it my all in effort, commitment and applying myself as best I can. This includes working around my strengths and weaknesses.

I will treat everyone with respect. I will strive to keep all interractions comfortable, friendly and productive. I believe the main way to do this will be by sharing our goals, in general and for the intteraction. Whether that is peer coding, a lesson, or generally hanging out, the ideal outcome is having an underlining layer of respect and integrity with everything we do at Dev Academy.

I will reach out for help if things are building up, and I can't deal with them. I normally deal with build ups by getting outside, doing exercise, taking a break and or generally taking a moment to reflect.

I will seek help if I'm stuck for 20-30mins or more, I will ask via my cohort or facilitators, where ever I think will give the best result. Generally I'll ask my cohort, or fellow students, unless it's really difficult. Normally though the facilitators have already noticed if it's a difficult problem.

My expectations for the facilitators

I expect what everyone expects from the facilitation team, for them to be fair, and help me when they can. To be clear about expectations, and communications around important topics.
